🚪 AUTOMATIC DOOR OPENER
Easy - 5 min
📋 Materials Required:
1 Wooden Door
2 Pressure Plates (Wooden or Stone)
2 Redstone Dust
4 Redstone Torches
Step 1: Prepare the Area
Step 1
Find a flat area and clear a 5×5 space for your door system.
■ ■ ■ ■ ■ ■ . . . ■ ■ . D . ■ ■ . . . ■ ■ ■ ■ ■ ■
Step 2: Place the Door
Step 2
Place your wooden door in the center of your cleared space. The door frame should be 2 blocks tall.
FRONT VIEW: SIDE VIEW: ┌─────┐ ┌─────┐ │ │ │ D D │ │ D │ │ D D │ │ │ └─────┘ └─────┘
Step 3: Place Pressure Plates
Step 3
Place one pressure plate on EACH side of the door (in front and behind). The player steps on either plate to activate.
FRONT: P . . . P . . D . . . . D . . BACK: P (pressure plates on front and back)
Step 4: Run Redstone Dust
Step 4
Place redstone dust from each pressure plate to the door. Connect the dust trails so they meet at the door's base.
R = Redstone Dust P - R - D - R - P | | R-------R
Step 5: Add Redstone Torches
Step 5
Place redstone torches on the blocks adjacent to the door to amplify the signal and trigger the door mechanism.
T = Redstone Torch P - R - T D T - R - P (door)
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Use stone pressure plates to only trigger for players, not mobs
Add more pressure plates on different sides for different entry points
Place the door flush with walls for a seamless look
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Don't place redstone directly on the door block - place it beside it
Make sure pressure plates are connected with redstone dust
The door needs power on BOTH sides to work properly
🔶 2×2 PISTON DOOR
Medium - 15 min
📋 Materials Required:
4 Pistons
4 Sticky Pistons
4 Blocks (any material for door panels)
1 Redstone Repeater (set to 3 ticks)
12 Redstone Dust
2 Pressure Plates OR 1 Button/Lever
Step 1: Build the Door Frame
Step 1
Create a 2×2 frame where your door will be. This is where the pistons will push blocks through.
TOP VIEW: ╔═══╗ ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ╚═══╝ Door opening = 2×2 blocks
Step 2: Place Pistons Around Door
Step 2
Place 4 regular pistons around the door opening. Place 4 sticky pistons ABOVE the regular pistons.
SIDE VIEW: ╔════════╗ ║ SP SP ║ (Sticky Pistons on top) ║ P P ║ (Regular Pistons) ║ ║ ║ DOOR ║ ║ ║ ╚════════╝
Step 3: Add Door Blocks
Step 3
Place 4 blocks on top of the sticky pistons. These will be pushed out to form the 2×2 door.
BLOCKS ARRANGEMENT: [B] [B] [SP][SP] [P] [P] Where: B = Block, SP = Sticky Piston, P = Piston
Step 4: Route Redstone
Step 4
Run redstone dust from your activation source (button/pressure plate) to all 8 pistons in a line.
REDSTONE PATH (top view): Button → R → R → R → R ↓ Pistons (all powered)
Step 5: Add Repeater for Timing
Step 5
Place a repeater set to 3 ticks delay in the redstone line. This ensures all pistons activate at the correct time.
Button → R → [Repeater: 3 ticks] → R → Pistons
Step 6: Add Activation Switch
Step 6
Place a button, lever, or pressure plate to activate the system. When activated, pistons push blocks to open/close the door.
BUTTON PLACEMENT: [BUTTON] → Redstone → [Piston Door]
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Use a lever for toggle doors or buttons for single-use
Add decorative blocks around pistons for better aesthetics
Use different block materials to match your building style
Add lighting inside the door opening for atmosphere
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Don't mix regular and sticky pistons incorrectly
Ensure all pistons receive power at the same time
Make sure blocks can freely move in and out
⚡ PRESSURE PLATE TRAP SYSTEM
Medium - 10 min
📋 Materials Required:
2 Pressure Plates
1 Piston
1 Redstone Repeater (set to 1 tick)
8 Redstone Dust
2 Redstone Torches
1 Block (sand, gravel, or concrete)
Step 1: Dig the Trap Floor
Step 1
Dig a 3×3 hole that is 2 blocks deep. This will be where the trap is triggered.
TOP VIEW: ┌─────┐ │ ■ ■ │ │ ■ ■ │ │ ■ ■ │ └─────┘ 2 blocks deep
Step 2: Place Pressure Plates
Step 2
Place one pressure plate on the bottom of the hole and one on top. Cover the bottom one with sand/gravel so it's hidden.
TRAP LAYOUT: ┌────────────┐ │ Pressure │ ← Visible (top) │ Plate │ │ SAND/DIRT │ ← Hidden beneath │ Pressure │ ← Bottom trigger │ Plate │ └────────────┘
Step 3: Build Redstone Circuit
Step 3
Connect the bottom pressure plate to a redstone line. Run it to a repeater, then to the piston mechanism.
CIRCUIT PATH: Pressure Plate → R → [Repeater] → R → Piston
Step 4: Set Up Piston Mechanism
Step 4
Place a piston facing UPWARD beneath the sand/gravel layer. When activated, it will push the block upward, triggering the top plate.
PISTON SETUP (side view): ┌──────────┐ │ Sand │ ← Will be pushed up │ Piston ↑ │ ← Pushes sand up │ │ └──────────┘
Step 5: Add Trigger Mechanism
Step 5
Connect the top pressure plate to a final redstone circuit that triggers your trap (falling sand, suffocation, or other damage).
TRAP ACTIVATION: Top Plate → R → Final Trap Mechanism Could be: Suffocation, Falling Damage, or Custom Trap
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Use falling sand/gravel for suffocation damage
Add a delay with repeaters to give players a chance to escape
Camouflage the trap with natural-looking blocks
Test the trap on animals before using on players
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Make sure the bottom pressure plate is accessible only to enemies
Don't make the trap too obvious or players will avoid it
Test timing carefully to ensure proper activation
🔪 AUTOMATIC MOB GRINDER
Hard - 30 min
📋 Materials Required:
16 Pistons
32 Redstone Dust
4 Repeaters (set to different ticks)
4 Levers or Buttons
Many building blocks
Step 1: Build the Collection Chamber
Step 1
Create a large chamber (8×8×4) where mobs will be collected. This should be dark to spawn mobs.
TOP VIEW: ╔════════╗ ║ ░░░░░░ ║ ║ ░░░░░░ ║ ║ ░░░░░░ ║ ║ ░░░░░░ ║ ╚════════╝ ░ = Dark spawn area
Step 2: Create the Killing Floor
Step 2
Build a separate chamber below the collection area. This is where mobs will be killed (damage by fall or other means).
SIDE VIEW: ┌────────┐ │ Mobs │ ← Collection │ here │ ├────────┤ ← 5+ block drop │ DANGER │ ← Killing area │ ZONE │ └────────┘
Step 3: Install Pusher Pistons
Step 3
Install pistons on 3 sides of the collection chamber, all facing inward. These will push mobs together toward the drop hole.
TOP VIEW (Pistons marked with arrows): ╔══════════╗ ║ → Mobs ← ║ ║ ↓ ↓ ║ ║ ↓Hole↓ ↓ ║ ╚══════════╝
Step 4: Create Drop Hole
Step 4
Place a 2×2 hole in the center of the collection chamber floor where mobs will fall to their death (5+ blocks for damage).
DROP HOLE: ╔═══════╗ ║ ░ ░ ░ ║ ║ ░ ░ ░ ║ ║ ░ ░ ░ ║ ║ ░[◇◇]░ ║ ║ ░[◇◇]░ ║ ║ └─────┘ ◇ = 2×2 hole
Step 5: Wire Pistons to Repeaters
Step 5
Connect all pusher pistons to repeaters set at different delays (1, 2, 3, 4 ticks). This staggers the piston pushes.
REPEATER SETUP: Button → [Repeater 1] → Pistons Group A → [Repeater 2] → Pistons Group B → [Repeater 3] → Pistons Group C → [Repeater 4] → Pistons Group D
Step 6: Test and Activate
Step 6
Activate the system with a lever. Mobs should be pushed toward center and fall through the hole to take damage.
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Make the chamber completely dark (light level 0) to maximize mob spawning
Use repeaters with staggered delays for smoother mob movement
Place the killing area 5-20 blocks below for optimal damage
Add hoppers below to collect dropped items
Use a lever to toggle the grinder on/off as needed
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Collection chamber must be completely dark or mobs won't spawn
Don't make the drop too low (over 50 blocks = overkill)
Ensure no light leaks into the chamber
Test mob spawning rates before finalizing
🌾 AUTOMATIC CROP FARM
Medium - 20 min
📋 Materials Required:
4 Pistons
1 Repeater (set to 2 ticks)
12 Redstone Dust
1 Lever
Water Bucket
Farmland blocks
Hoppers (4)
Chest
Step 1: Prepare the Farm Area
Step 1
Create a 10×10 flat area. Place water channels running through it to hydrate crops.
WATER LAYOUT: ╔══════════╗ ║ C C C C C║ ║ W C C C C║ ║ C C C C C║ ║ W C C C C║ ║ C C C C C║ ╚══════════╝ C = Crop, W = Water
Step 2: Plant Crops
Step 2
Plant wheat, potatoes, carrots, or other crops in the farmland. Wait for them to fully grow.
Step 3: Install Pistons for Harvesting
Step 3
Place 4 pistons in a line at ground level pointing toward the crops. These will break the mature crops.
PISTON PLACEMENT (top view): [P] [P] [P] [P] → → → Pistons push toward crops
Step 4: Add Water Flushing System
Step 4
Place water flow channels below pistons. When pistons break crops, water will carry items to hoppers.
WATER FLOW: [Crops] [Pistons] ↓ [Water] ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓ [Hoppers] → [Chest]
Step 5: Set Up Collection System
Step 5
Place 4 hoppers below the water channels, all connected to a central chest for item storage.
Step 6: Wire Redstone Circuit
Step 6
Connect pistons to a repeater (set to 2 ticks) and then to a lever. Activate the lever to harvest.
ACTIVATION: [Lever] → R → [Repeater: 2 tick] → [Pistons]
Step 7: Replanting (Optional)
Step 7
To fully automate, you can set up a dispenser to replant seeds. Connect it to a clock circuit.
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Use water channels to hydrate all crops (no manual watering needed)
Space pistons evenly across the farm for complete coverage
Use hoppers with filter systems to sort crops by type
Create a timer circuit to harvest on a schedule
Fully light the farm to prevent mob spawning
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Make sure water flows to hoppers, not away from them
Ensure crops are fully grown before harvesting
Don't forget to hydrate farmland (use water channels)
Check that hoppers are facing the correct direction
⬆️ PISTON-BASED ELEVATOR
Hard - 40 min
📋 Materials Required:
12 Sticky Pistons (per floor)
4 Regular Pistons (per floor)
4 Slime Blocks (or honey blocks)
20+ Redstone Dust
4 Repeaters (various delays)
2 Buttons or Levers
Step 1: Build the Elevator Column
Step 1
Create a 3×3 vertical column that's 10-20 blocks tall. This will be your elevator shaft.
SIDE VIEW: ╔═══╗ ║ ░ ║ ← Top floor ╠═══╣ ║ ░ ║ ← Elevator shaft ║ ░ ║ ║ ░ ║ ║ ░ ║ ║ ░ ║ ║ ░ ║ ╚═══╝ ← Ground floor
Step 2: Install Sticky Pistons
Step 2
Place sticky pistons on all 4 sides of the column, pointing inward. Repeat this pattern every 3 blocks vertically.
STICKY PISTON PATTERN (top view): ╔═══════╗ ║ → ← ║ ║ ↑ ↓ ║ ║ [S] ║ ║ ↓ ↑ ║ ║ ← → ║ ╚═══════╝
Step 3: Add Slime Blocks
Step 3
Place slime blocks attached to the sticky pistons. These will be what you stand on inside the elevator.
SLIME PLACEMENT: ╔═══╗ ║[S]║ ← Slime block (player stands here) ║ █ ║ ║ █ ║ ║[S]║ ← Next slime block ╚═══╝
Step 4: Create Activation Switches
Step 4
Place buttons at the bottom and top of the elevator. Pressing them should move the elevator up or down.
Step 5: Wire the Redstone
Step 5
Connect buttons to repeaters set at staggered delays (1, 2, 3, 4 ticks). This synchronized piston activation creates smooth movement.
CIRCUIT (simplified): [Button UP] → [R:1] → Piston Group 1 → [R:2] → Piston Group 2 → [R:3] → Piston Group 3 → [R:4] → Piston Group 4
Step 6: Test Movement
Step 6
Press the buttons to test. Pistons should extend/retract in sequence, moving the elevator up/down smoothly.
💡 Tips for Better Performance:
Use honey blocks instead of slime for quieter operation
Create multiple elevator shafts for better performance in servers
Add decorative blocks around the shaft for aesthetics
Use repeaters with different delays for smooth acceleration/deceleration
Add lighting inside the shaft to prevent mob spawning
⚠ Common Mistakes:
Don't forget to attach slime blocks to sticky pistons
Ensure all pistons activate with proper timing delays
Make the shaft exactly 3×3 for optimal piston coverage
Test thoroughly to avoid getting stuck mid-elevator
